PDEOncology

A browser-based platform for simulating tumor drug penetration using reaction-diffusion partial differential equations. Designed for researchers, educators, and clinicians who want to explore how drug physicochemical properties and tumor microenvironment interact to determine therapeutic efficacy.

∂C/∂t = ∇·(D(x,y)∇C) − λC − k·ρ(x,y)·C
FDM · FTCS scheme 80 × 80 grid 20-frame animation Claude API client-side JS open source

Motivation

Drug resistance and treatment failure in solid tumors are often not purely pharmacological — they are physical. Even potent drugs can fail to reach the tumor core due to elevated interstitial fluid pressure (IFP), dense extracellular matrix, and poor vascularisation. These barriers are especially pronounced in pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma, glioblastoma, and triple-negative breast cancer.

PDEOncology provides an accessible simulation environment to visualise these phenomena. By adjusting diffusion coefficient D, degradation rate λ, and uptake rate k, users can intuitively explore how molecular weight, metabolic stability, and receptor expression shape drug distribution — without requiring a computational background.

The AI integration (Claude API) goes one step further: users can describe a drug and tumor in plain English and receive biophysically grounded PDE parameters derived from published literature, enabling rapid hypothesis generation.

How it works

⬡

Numerical method

Explicit finite difference (FTCS scheme) on a uniform 2D grid (80 × 80 nodes, representing a 1 cm × 1 cm tissue cross-section). Stability is enforced via the CFL condition:

dt ≤ dx² / (4D)
◉

Tumor geometry

Circular tumor geometry with a radially-graded cell density field ρ(x,y). Diffusion coefficient D attenuated in tumor core to simulate IFP and extracellular matrix barriers.

◈

AI integration

Uses Claude API to extract biophysically grounded PDE parameters from natural-language descriptions. Falls back to local database when offline.

▷

Delivery modes

IV Infusion — Left boundary maintained at C = 1.0, simulating systemic drug delivery with a blood-vessel boundary. Vascular ring — Concentration initialised on an annular ring surrounding the tumor. Intratumoral injection — Point source at tumor centre.

Quickstart

1
Pick a preset

Go to Simulation, select a drug and tumor type from the dropdowns. Parameters auto-fill from the built-in database.

2
Or use AI

Go to AI Drug Input, describe your drug + tumor in plain English. Claude fills the parameters automatically.

3
Run & animate

Click Run simulation. A 20-frame diffusion animation appears automatically — hit Play to watch drug spread over time.

4
Export

Go to Results to view analytics, read the auto-generated report, and export CSV or PNG.

Limitations & assumptions

PDEOncology is an educational and exploratory tool. Simplifications include: circular homogeneous geometry, 2D model, static density fields, no convective transport, normalised dimensionless parameters, first-order time accuracy.

Results are model approximations and should not be used for clinical decision-making.
